Beef Wellington is a luxurious, show-stopping dish that’s known for its impressive presentation and rich flavors. At its core, it features a tender cut of beef—usually a center-cut fillet or tenderloin—perfectly seared to develop a savory crust. The beef is then coated in a layer of rich, earthy mushroom duxelles, which adds moisture and depth to the dish. Sometimes, a thin layer of prosciutto or pâté is added for extra flavor. The entire package is wrapped in a buttery, golden puff pastry, which bakes to a crisp, flaky texture. When cut, the beef is perfectly rare to medium-rare, complemented by the savory mushrooms and crisp pastry. Beef Wellington is typically served with a flavorful sauce, like a red wine reduction or béarnaise, to elevate the dish even further. It’s a true indulgence and a centerpiece perfect for special occasions.

Ingridients

  • Beef Tenderloin (Center Cut) – A high-quality, tender piece of beef, usually around 2 pounds.
  • Mushrooms – Typically cremini, shiitake, or button mushrooms, finely chopped for the duxelles.
  • Prosciutto – Thinly sliced, to wrap the beef and add flavor.
  • Pâté (Optional) – Often foie gras or a mushroom pâté, spread on the beef before wrapping.
  • Puff Pastry – Enough to fully enclose the beef and other layers.
  • Eggs – For an egg wash to give the pastry a golden, shiny finish.
  • Butter – To sauté the mushrooms and help sear the beef.
  • Garlic – For flavoring the mushrooms.
  • Shallots – To add depth to the mushroom duxelles.
  • Herbs – Fresh thyme and rosemary to season the beef.
  • Salt & Pepper – To taste, for seasoning the beef and other components.

Steps to follow!

To make Beef Wellington, start by searing a seasoned beef tenderloin in a hot pan until browned on all sides. Let it cool, then refrigerate. For the mushroom duxelles, finely chop mushrooms and sauté with garlic, shallots, and herbs until the mixture is dry. Cool completely. Lay prosciutto slices on plastic wrap, spread pâté (if desired), and evenly distribute the mushroom mixture on top. Roll the beef in the prosciutto, wrap tightly, and chill again. Roll out puff pastry and brush the beef with mustard (optional), then wrap it in the pastry, sealing edges. Brush with egg wash and refrigerate again. Preheat the oven to 400°F and bake the Wellington for 30-40 minutes, until the pastry is golden and the beef reaches your desired doneness. Let it rest before slicing. Serve with a rich red wine sauce made by reducing wine and beef stock with butter for a velvety finish.
